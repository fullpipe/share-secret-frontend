How it works?

For example, you want to share your database root password with your DevOps department. Enter your password on /seal page and split it into few shares. You could control how many shares will be created and how many of them will be required to restore the password.

After the split, you send shares to people in your department. One share for one person. Then remove the original database password from everywhere.

When it comes to making some “root” manipulations with your database, you start a temporary /unseal session. Enter your share and send the unseal link to other people from the DevOps department. When the required number of keepers enter their shares, the password will be unsealed. Everyone who enters a valid share will see an unsealed database password. It is required to enter a valid share to see the password. If someone enters an invalid or empty share, the unseal session will be failed and you have to start over with a new unseal session.

We do not keep any data on the server. Only during unseal session in memory we keep entered shares which will be deleted after the unseal session is over or failed.
